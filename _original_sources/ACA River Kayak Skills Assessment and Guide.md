
ACA River Kayak Skills Assessment and Guide  
Procedure and Guide for use during Skills Assessment:  
● Candidates must be assessed by a certified ACA instructor, Instructor-Trainer or Instructor Trainer Educator. ● Venues chosen for assessment must be appropriate to the assessment level being sought by the candidate; i.e., a Level 3 assessment is to be done in a class I/II venue. 
● Assessors can only offer assessments up to and including their own level of certification; i.e., a Level 3 instructor/assessor cannot assess candidates at Level 4. 
● Assessors are to use the skills rubric chart below to assess candidates in Technical Knowledge, Flat Water Strokes and Skills, Moving Water Skills, and Risk Assessment and Management. 
Candidates must meet the standard in each of the required skills per the assessment level they are seeking; i.e. candidates seeking  a Level 4 assessment must be observed performing all of the skills in the Level 4 description in the appropriate venues. Use the  descriptions in the rubric chart as a guide for assessments, then rate the candidates’ performance in the candidate rating box per  level description; i.e., candidate performs at the Level 2 description, list 2 in candidate rating. Please provide narrative candidate  feedback for each of the four categories, highlighting strengths and offering next steps for growth. If a candidate is below standard  for any Assessed Skill, they are to be provided an action plan that outlines areas for practice and a recommendation of when to  return for the assessment. 
Procedure and Guide for use during Instructor Certification Exams: This skills rubric may also be used to assess Instructor  Candidates for certification. Instructor Candidates should have a deeper level of understanding and be able to teach each skill.  Instructor Candidates are expected to be able to perform each skill with minimal to no instruction when presenting for an instructor  course. At the conclusion of the certification exam, Certified Instructors are expected to be able to perform, role model, teach and  troubleshoot every skill within all levels of their certification. 
Technical Knowledge 
This includes general knowledge of paddlesport safety, equipment, paddling theory, river reading, river running strategies, and rescue. Including an ability to explain these concepts in layperson’s terms. Experience is a key factor in Technical Knowledge. 
Evaluation System: Many of these concepts and skills can be assessed via conversations, safety talks and observations of candidates’ readiness for level of assessment, i.e. has proper gear for the activity and venue, gear is maintained and in good order. 
Meets Standards: Candidates have the appropriate understanding and depth of knowledge for their assessment level and can communicate it effectively. 
Below Standards: Lack of knowledge appropriate for the level of assessment sought. Lack of ability to explain concepts in layperson’s terms.

Flat Water Strokes and Skills 
Flat Water skills are the foundation to good paddling technique. Flat Water practice allows us to focus on stroke efficiency and improve technique without the distractions of moving current. 
Evaluation System: Observation of candidate paddling strokes and skills on flat water. Candidate demonstrates the following skills satisfactorily per level description. 
Meets Standards: Knowledge of skills and ability to meet the standard description for assessment level sought with engaged posture, core rotation, edge control, vision and mental tranquility. 
Below Standards: Lack of knowledge of skills. Poor posture, lack of core rotation and engagement, inability to control boat edges, lack of mental tranquility. Tippy and uncomfortable in the boat. Close mindedness about technique improvement.

Risk Assessment and Management 
Candidates can properly assess and manage risks associated with paddling at the level they are seeking for skills assessment. 
Evaluation System: Many of these concepts and skills can be assessed via conversations, safety talks and observations of candidates’ readiness for level of assessment, i.e. has proper gear for the activity and venue, gear is maintained and in good order. Candidates will also be observed performing rescue skills in the venue they seek assessment. Candidates can demonstrate understanding of rescue options that might be available, and appropriately prioritize techniques. Candidates consider the speed, simplicity and safety of rescue techniques. Ideal rescues are simple, safe and speedy. 
Meets Standards: Candidates have the appropriate understanding and depth of knowledge for their assessment level and can communicate it effectively. Candidates can perform skills to level description in the rubric. 
Below Standards: Lack of knowledge appropriate for the level of assessment sought. Lack of ability to perform skills with confidence and mental tranquility. Lack of ability to explain concepts in layperson’s terms.
